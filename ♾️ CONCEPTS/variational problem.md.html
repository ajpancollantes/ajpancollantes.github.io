Variational problem

(See @olver86 page 243)

Let $\Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^p$ be an open, connected subset with smooth boundary $\partial \Omega$. A variational problem consists of finding the extrema of a functional

$$ J[u]=\int_{\Omega}L(x,u^{(n)})dx $$

in some class of functions $u:\mathbb{R}^p\to \mathbb{R}^q$, where $L$ is called the Lagrangian of the variational problem $J$ and it is a smooth function of $x$, $u(x)$ and their derivatives. Indeed, the Lagrangian should be thought as a horizontal 1-form, instead of a function. See Lagrangian Mechanics#Jet space approach.

The search of the extrema of this functional can be shown to be equivalent (by means of the variational derivative) to the Euler-Lagrange equations.

In this context we have variational-Noether symmetrys, which have some relation to the generalized symmetries of the associated Euler-Lagrange equations.
