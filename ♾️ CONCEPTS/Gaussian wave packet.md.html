Gaussian wave packet

In a 2 spatial dimension context, a Gaussian wave packet is an initial condition for the Schrodinger equation represented as:

$$ \Psi(x,y,0) = A \exp\left(-\frac{(x-x_0)^2 + (y-y_0)^2}{4\sigma^2}\right) \exp\left(\frac{i}{\hbar}(p_{x0}x + p_{y0}y)\right) $$

Where:

  • $A$ is the normalization constant.

  • $\sigma$ is the width of the Gaussian packet.

  • $(x_0, y_0)$ is the initial position of the center of the packet.

  • $(p_{x0}, p_{y0})$ are the initial momenta in the x and y directions.
