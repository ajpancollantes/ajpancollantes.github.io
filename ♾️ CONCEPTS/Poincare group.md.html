Poincare group

The group $Poin(3,1)$ or $ISO(3,1)$ is the semidirect product of translations $\mathbb{R}^4$ with the Lorentz group $O(3,1)$:

$$ ISO(3,1)=\mathbb{R}^4 \rtimes O(3,1) $$

Poincar√© symmetry is the full symmetry of special relativity. It includes:

  • translations (displacements) in time and space, forming the abelian Lie group of translations on space-time;

  • rotations in space, forming the non-Abelian Lie group of three-dimensional rotations

  • boosts, or Lorentz transformations connecting two uniformly moving observers.

It substituted the Galilean group $Gal(3)$. See \section{Development of SR} at notasfisica.tex, and write it to obsidian.
