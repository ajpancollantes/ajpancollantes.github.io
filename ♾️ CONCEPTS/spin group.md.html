Spin groups

The spin group $Spin(n,m)$ is the double cover of the group $SO(n,m)$ (the special orthogonal group). The latter is the group of

Spin Groups:

  • The double cover of $SO(2)$ is $\text{Spin}(2)$, which is isomorphic to $U(1)=SU(1)$. They are the unit complex numbers.

  • The double cover of $SO(3)$ is given by $Spin(3)$. This is isomorphic to $SU(2)$ and can also be represented by unit quaternions.

  • The double cover of $SO^+(1,3)$ is $Spin(1,3)$, which is equivalent to $SL(2, \mathbb{C})$.
