Definition

A manifold $M$ together with a Riemannian metric. It is called "pseudo" if we do not require the Riemannian metric to be definite positive.

They are, in some sense, metric spaces: we can measure lengths. If their geodesic curves could be prolonged all you want, we say that $M$ is a complete manifold. So we can compute volumes in a natural way, and therefore we have a distinguished volume form in it.

Interpretation as a Cartan geometry

It can be interpreted as a Cartan geometry.
