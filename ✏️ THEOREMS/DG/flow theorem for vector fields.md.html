Esto está en [Warner 1983] prop 1.48:

Proposition

Sea $X$ un campo de vectores en una variedad $M$. Para cada $p \in M$ existen $a(p),b(p) \in \mathbb{R} \cup \{\pm \infty\}$ y una curva diferenciable

$$ \gamma_p: (a(p), b(p)) \longrightarrow M $$

tal que:

1. $0 \in (a(p), b(p))$ y $\gamma_p(0)=p$

2. $\gamma_p$ es una curva integral de $X$ (es decir, $d(\gamma_p)_s(\frac{d}{dt})=X(\gamma_p(s))$)

3. Uniqueness: Si $\mu:(c,d) \longrightarrow M$ es otra curva satisfaciendo las condiciones anteriores entonces $(c,d) \subseteq (a(p), b(p))$ y $\gamma_p|_{(c,d)}=\mu$.

4. Para cada $t \in \mathbb{R}$ definimos una transformación $X_t$ con dominio $D_t=\{q \in M: t \in (a(q),b(q))\}$ mediante la expresión $X_t(q)=\gamma_q(t)$, de manera que para cada $p\in M$ existe un entorno abierto $V$ y un $\epsilon >0$ tal que la aplicación

$$ (t,q)\longrightarrow X_t(q) $$

está definida en $(\epsilon, \epsilon)\times V$. It is called flow of $X$ and it is usually denoted by $\phi_X(t,q).$

5. $D_t$ es abierto para cada $t$.

6. $\bigcup_{t>0} D_t =M$.

7. $X_t: D_t\longrightarrow D_{-t}$ es un difeomorfismo con inversa $X_{-t}$.

8. Sean $s,t \in \mathbb{R}$. El dominio de $X_s \circ X_t$ está contenido en $D_{t+s}$ (pero en general no es igual). Lo es si $s$ y $t$ tienen el mismo signo. Más aún, en el dominio de $X_s \circ X_t$, se verifica que $X_s \circ X_t=X_{t+s}$. Therefore we have a local group of transformations.

Important facts

Proposition

Let $X$ be a vector field, and $k$ a constant. Let's denote $\phi_X$ and $\phi_{kX}$the flow of $X$ and $kX$ respectively. It is satisfied that:

$$ \phi_{kX}(t,q)=\phi_{X}(kt,q). $$

Proof

We only have to check that $\hat{\phi}(t,q)=\phi_{X}(kt,q)$ satisfies 1. and 2. for the vector field $kX$, and then we will have the result. In fact:

1. $\hat{\phi}(0,q)=\phi_{X}(0,q)=q$.

2. $\hat{\phi}(t,q)$ are integral curves of $kX$:

$$ \frac{d}{dt}\hat{\phi}(t,q)=\frac{d}{dt}\phi_{X}(kt,q)=k X_{\phi_{X}(kt,q)} $$

$\blacksquare$

Jacobian of the flow of a vector field

Proposition

Let $X$ be a vector field and $\phi_t(p)$ its flow. For every $t$ the map $\phi_t(-)$ is a diffeomorphism, so we can consider its Jacobian

$$ J(t,x):=det(D_x \phi_t(x)). $$

It is satisfied that

$$ \frac{d}{dt}|_{t=0} J(t,x)=\mbox{div}X (x) $$

where $\mbox{div}$ is the divergence.

Proof

See this answer in Mathstackexchange. $\blacksquare$

Even more,

$$ J(t,x)=e^{\int_0^t \mbox{div}X(\phi_s(x))ds}. $$
