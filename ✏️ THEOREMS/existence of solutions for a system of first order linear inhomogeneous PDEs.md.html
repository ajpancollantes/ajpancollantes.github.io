Existence of solutions for system of first order linear inhomogeneous PDEs

See my question on MO.

For $i=1,\ldots, r$, let $Z_i$ be $r$ linearly independent vector fields defined on an open subset $U$ of $\mathbb{R}^n$, $r $$ Z_i(f)=\lambda_i, \quad i=1,\ldots,r. $$

Question: What hypothesis do we need to assure the local existence of a solution $f$?

According to Bryant answer (remark at the end): it can be shown the existence using the involutivity of $\{Z_i\}$, by using Frobenius theorem.

But also it can be proven in general, not only for linear inhomogeneous PDEs, but for first order PDEs whenever they give rise to a "involutive submanifold" $\Sigma$ of the first order jet bundle. I don't know yet what is an involutive manifold.
