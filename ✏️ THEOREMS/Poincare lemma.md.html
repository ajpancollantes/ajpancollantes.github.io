Poincare lemma

The Poincaré lemma states that every closed differential form is locally exact.

Suppose X is a smooth manifold, Ωk(X) is the set of smooth differential k-forms on X, and suppose ω is a closed form in Ωk(X) for some k>0. Then for every x∈X there is a neighbourhood U⊂X, and a (k−1)-form η∈Ωk−1(U) , such that dη=ι∗ω, where ι is the inclusion ι:U↪X.

If X is contractible space, this η exists globally; there exists a (k−1)-form η∈Ωk−1(X) such that dη=ω.

For 1-forms, you only need $X$ to be simply connected.
