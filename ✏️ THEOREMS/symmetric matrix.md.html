Symmetric matrix

I think that a symmetric matrix $A$ is always of the form

$$ A=V^t D V $$

where $V$ is a orthogonal matrix and $D$ is a diagonal one. This is a version of the spectral theorem.

The meaning is that symmetric matrices correspond to scale changes. While diagonal matrices encode scale changes in the main axis, a general symmetric matrix represents a scale change in another axis, and $V$ is the transformation of axis. In a sense, they are a kind of generalization of diagonal matrices.

Symmetric matrices are, therefore, always diagonalizable and have orthogonal eigenvectors. This is related to self adjoint operators in finite dimensional Hilbert spaces.

Keep an eye: is not the same as symmetric operator, but is related.
