The same ODE in two jet spaces

Una ODE de orden $m$ se puede visualizar tanto en el jet bundle $J^{m-1}(\mathbb{R},\mathbb{R})$ como en el $J^{m}(\mathbb{R},\mathbb{R})$. El dibujo sería:

siendo

$$ i: (x,u,u_1,u_2) \longmapsto (x,u,u_1,u_2,F(x,u,u_1,u_2)) $$

El campo $A$ pasa a $i_* (A)$ definido solo en la imagen pero además tendríamos definido en todo $J^{m}(\mathbb{R},\mathbb{R})$ el campo

$$ \widetilde{A}=\partial x+u_1 \partial u+u_2 \partial u_1+u_3\partial u_2+D_x(F)\partial u_3 $$

tal que

$$ \widetilde{A}|_{\{u_3=F\}}=i_*(A) $$

Suppose we have a Lie point symmetry $X$ of the ODE. We have the prolongations

$$ X^{(m-1)}=\xi \partial x+\eta^0 \partial u+\sum^{m-1}\eta^k \partial u_k $$

prolonged up to $(m-1)$th order, and

$$ X^{(m)}=\xi \partial x+\eta^0 \partial u+\sum^{m} \eta^k \partial u_k= $$ $$ =\xi \partial x+\eta^0 \partial u+\sum^{m-1} \eta^k \partial u_k+(D_x \eta^{m-1}-u_m D_x \xi)\partial u_m $$

up to $m$th order.

And we can wonder, what is $i_*(X^{(m-1)})$?

$$ i_*(X^{(m-1)})=\xi\partial x+\eta^0 \partial u+\eta^1\partial u_1+\eta^2 \partial u_2+ X^{(m-1)}(F)\partial u_3 $$

If we restrict to $\{u_3=F\}$, do we have that

$$ X^{(m-1)}(F)=(D_x \eta^{m-1}-u_3 D_x \xi) $$

???

It must be true that

$$ X^{(m)}(u_3-F)=0 $$

(in order to be a symmetry) so

$$ (D_x \eta^{m-1}-u_3 D_x \xi)-X^{(m-1)}(F)=0 $$

and the answer is YES.

And therefore $X^{(m)}$ is a vector field in $J^m$ that extend $i_*(X^{(m-1)})$.
