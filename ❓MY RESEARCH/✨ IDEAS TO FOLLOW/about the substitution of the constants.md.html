Lemma

Let $\mathcal{Z}$ be an involutive distribution such that its dual description is given by the completely integrable Pfaffian system $\mathcal{P}=\{\omega_1,\ldots,\omega_r\}$ in an open set $U\subseteq \mathbb{R}^n$. And let $\sigma \in \Omega^1(U)$ be a 1-form. Then $\sigma \in \mathcal{P}$ if and only if for every parametrization

$$ i:V\subseteq \mathbb{R}^{n-r}\to U $$

of a locally maximal integral submanifold of $\mathcal{P}$, then $i^*(\sigma)=0$.

Proof

One direction is trivial.

For the converse, what I call lemma of belongingness to ideal establishes that if $\sigma(V)=0$ for every $V\in \mathcal{Z}$ then $\sigma \in \mathcal{P}$. But this condition is implied by the hypothesis.$\blacksquare$
