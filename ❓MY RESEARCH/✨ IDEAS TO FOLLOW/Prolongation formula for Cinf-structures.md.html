More ideas on the prolongation formula for cinf-symmetry of distributions:

If a vector field $X$ satisfies the prolongation formula for vector fields then it is a symmetry of the Cartan distribution. But also it is known that a vector field satisfying the prolongation formula for dynamical Cinf-symmetries although is not a symmetry of the Cartan distribution, it "$\lambda$-preserves" the Cartan distribution ([Gaeta 2004]). It is the same as saying that $X$ is a Cinf-symmetry of the Cartan distribution? I think so.

On the other hand, in a solvable structure we have symmetries of distributions, all of them containing in some sense the Cartan distribution...
