Relation between $\sigma$-symmetries and cinf-structures

See [Maple 021] and [xournal 122].

Definition of sigma-prolongation

Let $M\subseteq \mathbb{R}^2$ be the phase space of 1 independent variable $x$ and 1 dependent variable $u$, and $J^m M$ the corresponding jet bundle.

Let $\sigma=\left\{\sigma_{i}^{j} \equiv \sigma_{i j}, i, j=1, \ldots, r\right\}$ be a set of smooth functions on $J^1 M$.

Given $X_1, \ldots, X_r \in \mathfrak{X}(M)$, we say that $Y_1, \ldots, Y_r \in \mathfrak{X}(M^{(m)})$ are their $\sigma$-prolongation if provided that

$$ X_i=\xi_i \partial x+\eta_i^0 \partial u $$

with $\xi_i, \eta_i^0 \in \mathcal{C}^{\infty}(M)$, we have that

$$ Y_i=\xi_i \partial x+\eta_i^0 \partial u+\sum_k \eta_i^k \partial u_k $$

where

$$ \eta_i^{k+1}=D_x\eta_i^k-u_{k+1}D_x \xi_i+\sum_j \sigma_{i}^j(\eta_j^{k}-u_{k+1}\xi_j). $$

Characterization

The lemma 1 and the subsequent remark in [Cicogna 2012] establishes that this is equivalent to

$$ [Y_i,D_x]=\sum_j \sigma_{ij} Y_j+\rho D_x \tag{1} $$

The prolonged fields $Y_j$ can be in involution or not.

Two results for visualizing sigma-prolongations

The first one is Theorem 5 in Cicogna_2012:

Theorem

Let $\{X_i\}$ be vector fields in $M$, and $\{Y_i\}$ be their $\sigma$-prolongations. The $\mathcal{C}^{\infty}(M)$-module generated by $\{Y_i\}$ is the same as the one generated by the standard prolongations $\{Z_i\}$ of some $\{W_i\}$ which are linear combination of $\{X_i\}$ with a function matrix $\{A_{ij}\in \mathcal{C}^{\infty}(M)\}$ satisfying

$$ D_x A=A\sigma. $$

$\blacksquare$

Also in [Cicogna_2012] page 12 there is a lemma that let us to "see" what are sigma prolonged vector fields:

Lemma

Let $\{W_i\}$ be a set of vector fields on $M$ and $\{Z_i\}$ their standard prolongations. Consider linear combinations of the latter by means of a non-singular matrix function $B:M\to \mbox{GL}(n)$:

$$ Y_i=\sum_j B_i^j Z_j. $$

Then $\{Y_i\}$ is a set of $\sigma$-prolonged vector fields with $\sigma$ matrix

$$ \sigma_i^j=B^{-1}(D_x B). $$

$\blacksquare$

In the case of one single vector $W$, if we prolong in the standard way to $Z$ and then multiply by non-vanishing function $h$ we obtain a vector field $Y=hZ$ which is a $\lambda$-prolonged vector field, being $\lambda=\frac{D_x(h)}{h}$, which coincides with Lemma 5.1 in [Muriel 2001].

My ideas

Suppose we can change $D_x$ by $A$, the vector field of an ODE. Then a quasi-solvable structure $Y_1, \ldots,Y_r$ would satisfy condition (1) and therefore would be a kind of set of $\sigma$-prolonged vector fields (in a _generalized sense_, i.e. we would have two first infinitesimals depending on the derivatives) and if we restrict to a quasi-solvable structure of "vertical vector fields" (they don't have the $\partial x$ component) then they are in involution.

Therefore probably we could write a prolongation formula for these vector fields, that must be more simple than that of the $\sigma$-prolongation.
