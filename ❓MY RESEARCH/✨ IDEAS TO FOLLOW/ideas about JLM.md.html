Personal research about JLM

Jacobi last multipliers and volume

I have a result in xournal_155 page 1 that says that we can construct a JLM with the inverse of some kind of volume computed along the flow of the given vector field.

I think that every JLM is of this type.

Even more, I think that, taking into account this result on the Jacobian of the flow and xournal_155 page 5 we have something like this:

$$ M(q)=\dfrac{F(q_0)}{e^{\int_0^{t_0} \mbox{div}X(\phi_s(q_0))ds}} $$

where the notation is as follows: for every $q\in \mathbb{R}^n$ there is $q_0,t_0$ such that $q=\phi_{t_0}(q_0)$ being $\phi$ the flow of the vector field $X$ and $q_0\in K$, for a cross section $K$ of $\phi$.

Ohh: it is the same as Theorem 5 in @berrone2003inverse.
