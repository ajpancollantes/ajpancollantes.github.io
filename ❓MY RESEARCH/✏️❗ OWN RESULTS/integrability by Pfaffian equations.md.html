Theorem

Given an involutive distribution $\mathcal{Z}=\mathcal{S}(\{Z_1,\ldots,Z_r\})$ and a cinf-structure $\langle \omega_1,\ldots, \omega_{n-r}\rangle$ for $\mathcal{Z}$ we can find the integral manifolds of $\mathcal{Z}$ by solving $n-r$ completely integrable Pfaffian equations.

Remarks

  • La dificultad sigue estando en encontrar los integrating factors. Al menos sabemos que hay una relación inversa entre los integrating factors y las symmetrising factors.

  • Tengo la hipótesis de que se podría refinar cualquier cinf-structure para obtener otra en la cual se pueda usar el método atrás-adelante.

  • En orden 2 coincide con la reducción mediante $\lambda$-simetrías de Conchi.

Proof

Let's consider $p\in M$, we will look for the integral manifold passing through $p$. We can take a local coordinate chart $\left(U, \varphi\equiv(x_1,\ldots,x_n)\right)$ around $p$ and work in $\varphi(U)\subseteq \mathbb{R}^n$. In what follows, we will use the same symbols $p$, $\omega_j$ ($j=1,\ldots,n-r$), $Z_i $ ($ i=1,\ldots,r$)

and $\mathcal{Z}$ for the corresponding objects defined in $ \varphi(U)$, for notational simplicity.

We are going to proceed by induction on the dimension $ n $ of the manifold $M$ but starting at $r+1$. First, if $\operatorname{dim}(M)=r+1$ and we have a quasi-solvable structure $\langle \omega_1 \rangle$, the ideal $\mathcal{I}(\{\omega_{1}\})$ is a differential ideal, so $d\omega_1 \wedge \omega_1=0$ and the Pfaffian equation

$$ \omega_1\equiv 0 $$

is completely integrable. Let

  • $F$ be a solution, the submanifold $N=\{x\in \varphi(U): F(x)=c\}$, where $c=F(p)$, is an integral submanifold of $\mathcal{Z}$ through $p$ that can be pulled back to $U$.

Now, suppose the result is true for manifolds of dimension $n-1$ and let's check for dimension $n$. The ideal $\mathcal{I}(\{\omega_{n-r}\})$ is, again, a differential ideal, so we have the completely integrable Pfaffian equation

$$ \omega_{n-r}\equiv 0. $$

Let $F$ be a solution of it, assuming without lost of generality that $\frac{\partial F}{\partial x_n} \neq 0$, we can isolate $x_n $ in $F(x_1,\ldots,x_n)=c$

$$ x_n=\phi(x_1,\ldots,x_{n-1},c), $$

where $c=F(p) $ and consider the map

$$ \begin{array}{rccc} \iota: & \tilde{N} &\longrightarrow & \varphi(U)\\ &(x_1,x_2, \ldots,x_{n-1}) & \longmapsto & (x_1,x_2, \ldots,x_{n-1}, \phi(x_1,\ldots,x_{n-1},c)) \end{array} $$

for a suitable domain $\tilde{N} \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{n-1}$. This map is a diffeomorphism from $\tilde{N}$ to $N:=\{q\in \varphi(U):F(q)=c\}$, and since the vector fields $Z_i$ are tangent to $N$ everywhere, it is well defined the pushforward

$$ \widetilde{Z}_i:=(\iota^{-1})_*(Z_i) $$

for the restriction of $Z_i$ to $N$. We define, also

$$ \widetilde{\omega}_j:=\iota^*(\omega_j) \text{ for } j=1,\ldots,n-1. $$

Observe that $\widetilde{\omega}_{n-r}=0$ and that $\langle\widetilde{\omega}_1,\ldots,\widetilde{\omega}_{n-r-1} \rangle$ constitutes a quasi-solvable structure for $\widetilde{\mathcal{Z}}:=\mathcal{S}(\widetilde{Z}_1,\ldots,\widetilde{Z}_r)$.

If we call

$$ \widetilde{p}:=\iota^{-1}(p), $$

we can find, by induction hypothesis, an integral submanifold $\widetilde{S}$ for $\widetilde{\mathcal{Z}}$ passing through $\tilde{p}$, by solving $n-1-r$ completely integrable Pfaffian equations. Suppose we have managed to arrive at a parametrization $\sigma$ of $\widetilde{S}$, then $\iota \circ \sigma$ is a parametrization of the manifold $S:=\iota(\widetilde{S})$, which we claim is an integral submanifold of $\mathcal{Z}$ passing through $p$.

First, it is obvious that $S$ passes through $p$. And, on the other hand, given $q=\iota(\widetilde{q})\in S$ and $V\in T_{q}S$ it is clear that $(\iota^{-1})_*(V)=\sum_{i=1}^r h_i \cdot \widetilde{{Z}}_{i\widetilde{q}}$ for some $h_i \in \mathbb{R}$, since $\widetilde{S}$ is an integral submanifold of $\widetilde{\mathcal{Z}}$. Therefore

$$ V=\iota_*((\iota^{-1})_*(V))= \iota_*(\sum_{i=1}^r h_i \cdot {\widetilde{Z}}_{i\widetilde{q}})= \iota_*(\sum_{i=1}^r h_i \cdot (\iota^{-1})_*({Z_i}_{q}))=\sum_{i=1}^r h_i \cdot {Z_i}_{q}, $$

so $V\in \mathcal{Z}_{q}$.
