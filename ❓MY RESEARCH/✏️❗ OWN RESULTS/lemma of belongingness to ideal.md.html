Lemma: belongingness to the ideal

Given a distribution $D$ with dual description of the distribution $D^{\circ}=\mathcal{S}({\omega_1}, \ldots, {\omega_{n-r}} \})$, we denote

$$ \mathcal{I}(D)=\mathcal{I}(\{{\omega_1}, \ldots, {\omega_{n-r}} \}) $$

the algebraic ideal generated by $\omega_1,\ldots, \omega_{n-r}$.

For any $\omega \in \mathcal{I}(D)$, and for any $V_1, \ldots, V_k \in \Gamma(M,D)$ we have

$$ \omega(V_1,\ldots,V_k)=0. $$

The converse is also true:

Lema

If a differential $k$-form $\omega$ is such that

$$ \omega(V_1,\ldots,V_k)=0 \quad (1) $$

for any $V_1, \ldots, V_k \in \Gamma(M,D)$ then $\omega \in \mathcal{I}(D)$.

Proof

Consider a frame

$$ \{ X_1, \ldots, X_r,Y_1,\ldots,Y_{n-r}\} $$

and its dual coframe:

$$ \{ \delta_1, \ldots, \delta_r,\omega_1,\ldots,\omega_{n-r}\}, $$

associated to the distribution $D$ as in the discussion above. The form $\omega$ can be expressed as a sum of terms each of which consists on the wedge product of $k$ 1-forms of the coframe, that is:

$$ \omega= \sum h_J\cdot \delta_{i_1}\wedge \cdots\wedge \delta_{i_l}\wedge \omega_{j_1}\wedge \cdots\wedge \omega{j_m} $$

where

  • $l+m=k$

  • $1\leq i_1 <\cdots i_l\leq r$

  • $1\leq j_1 <\cdots j_m \leq n-r$

  • $J=(i_1,\ldots,i_l;j_1,\ldots,j_m)$

  • $h_J \in \mathcal{C}^{\infty}(M)$

If we select $k$ vector fields out of $\{X_1, \ldots, X_r\}$, say $X_{h_1},\ldots, X_{h_k}$, and substitute them into equation (1) in the ennunciate of the lema we obtain that

$$ h_J=0 $$

for $J=(h_1,\ldots,h_k;0)$. By replacing the ${r}\choose{k}$ possibilities in (1) we can show that all the summands of $\omega$ contains at least one factor $\omega_j$, so we can express

$$ \omega=\sum_{j=1}^{n-r} \alpha_j \wedge \omega_j $$

and thus, $\omega \in \mathcal{I}(D)$.$\blacksquare$
