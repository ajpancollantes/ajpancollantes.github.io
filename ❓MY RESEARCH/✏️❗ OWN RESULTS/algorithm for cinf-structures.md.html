Algorithm

Just like a solvable structure, a \cinf-structure $\langle X_1,\ldots, X_{n-r}\rangle$ of an involutive distribution $\mathcal{Z}$ can be used to find their integral manifolds by following a procedure which is similar to the case of solvable structures. This algorithm is as follows:

  • First, observe that the set $\{Z_1,\ldots,Z_r,X_1,\ldots,X_{n-r}\}$ constitute a frame on $U$, so we can consider the 1-forms $\omega_i$ constructed in \eqref{formas}. Thus we obtain a sequence of Pfaffian systems

$$ \begin{array}{l} \mathcal{P}_0=\mathcal{Z}^{\circ}=\mathcal{S}(\{\omega_1,\ldots,\omega_{n-r}\}),\\ \mathcal{P}_1=\left(\mathcal{Z}\oplus \mathcal{S}(\{X_1\})\right)^{\circ}=\mathcal{S}(\{\omega_2,\ldots,\omega_{n-r}\}), \\ \cdots\\ \mathcal{P}_{i}=\left(\mathcal{Z}\oplus \mathcal{S}(\{X_1,\ldots,X_i\})\right)^{\circ}=\mathcal{S}(\{\omega_{i+1},\ldots,\omega_{n-r}\}), \\ \cdots \\ \mathcal{P}_{n-r-1}=\left(\mathcal{Z}\oplus \mathcal{S}(\{X_1,\ldots,X_{n-r-1}\})\right)^{\circ}=\mathcal{S}(\{\omega_{n-r}\} ),\\ \end{array} $$

associated to the sequence of distributions $\mathcal{Z}\oplus \mathcal{S}(\{X_1,\ldots,X_{i}\})$, $i=1,\ldots,n-r-1$. These Pfaffian systems are completely integrable, since the corresponding distributions are involutive.

  • Then we look for a solution $I_{n-r}$ of the completely integrable Pfaffian equation $\mathcal{P}_{n-r-1}$, that is $\omega_{n-r}\equiv 0$, by solving the system of homogeneous linear first order PDEs given by

$$ dI_{n-r}\wedge \omega_{n-r}=0. $$

  • We fix an arbitrary constant $C_{n-r}\in \mathbb{R}$ and consider the integral manifold of $\mathcal{P}_{n-r-1}$ given by the level set $I_{n-r}=C_{n-r}$, which will be denoted by $\Sigma_{(C_{n-r})}$. Then we obtain the restriction of the Pfaffian systems $\mathcal{P}_{n-r-2}$ to $\Sigma_{(C_{n-r})}$,

$$ \mathcal{P}_{n-r-2}|_{\Sigma_{(C_{n-r})}}=\mathcal{S}(\{\omega_{n-r-1}|_{\Sigma_{(C_{n-r})}},\omega_{n-r}|_{\Sigma_{(C_{n-r})}}\}), $$

by pulling back with a suitable embedding $\iota_{n-r}:V_{n-1}\to \Sigma_{(C_{n-r})}$, with domain an open subset $V_{n-1}$ of $\mathbb{R}^{n-1}$. Observe than both $\iota_{n-r}$ and $V_{n-1}$ depend on the constant $C_{n-r}$.

  • The integral manifolds of the restricted Pfaffian system give rise to integral manifolds of the original ${\mathcal{P}}_{n-r-2}$ after composition with the embedding $\iota_{n-r}$. But now observe that $\mathcal{P}_{n-r-2}|_{\Sigma_{(C_{n-r})}}$ is a new Pfaffian equation (since $\omega_{n-r}|_{\Sigma_{(C_{n-r})}}=0$) defined on a space of one less variable. So we proceed as in step 2 by solving $\omega_{n-r-1}|_{\Sigma_{(C_{n-r})}}\equiv 0$, and using a solution to define the level sets $\Sigma_{(C_{n-r-1},C_{n-r})}$ with $C_{n-r-1}\in \mathbb R$.

  • In every iteration we solve the Pfaffian equation

$$ \omega_{i}|_{\Sigma_{(C_{i+1},\ldots,C_{n-r})}}\equiv 0. $$

A solution $I_{i}$ gives rise to level sets

$$ \Sigma_{(C_{i},C_{i+2},\ldots,C_{n-r})}=\{p\in \Sigma_{(C_{i+1},\ldots,C_{n-r})}:I_{i}(p)=C_{i}\}, \text{ with } C_i\in \mathbb R, $$

which are integral manifolds of the restriction of the Pfaffian system $\mathcal{P}_{i-1}$ to $\Sigma_{(C_{i+1},\ldots,C_{n-r})}$. The embedding of $\Sigma_{(C_{i},C_{i+2},\ldots,C_{n-r})}$ into $U$ are integral manifolds of $\mathcal{P}_{i-1}$.

Eventually, after $n-r$ iterations we arrive to integral manifolds of $\mathcal{P}_0=\mathcal{Z}^{\circ}$, which are the integral manifolds of $\mathcal{Z}$.
